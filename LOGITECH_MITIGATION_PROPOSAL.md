## 2. SITATION & BACKGROUND

### 2.1 Background

In light of the recent discovery of critical zero-day vulnerabilities affecting Logitech's technology ecosystem, this proposal establishes terms under which Digital Forensics Services Provider shall perform emergency remediation and forensic investigation services. The parties intend to create a framework for rapid response to security incidents affecting Logitech's organizational integrity and customer trust.

### 2.2 Intent

This Proposal establishes that Provider is engaged to provide digital forensics and zero-day remediation services without creating an attorney-client relationship, joint venture, or partnership. All services will be delivered in accordance with industry standards and best practices, with particular attention to the time-sensitive nature of zero-day vulnerability incidents.

### 2.3 Recognized Threats

The scope covers investigation and remediation of:
- **CVE-2026-41940**: Critical remote code execution vulnerability
- **CVE-2026-31431**: Authentication bypass vulnerability with privilege escalation potential

These vulnerabilities require immediate forensic assessment to determine:
- Exploitation status
- Data exposure scope
- Persistence mechanisms
- Attack timeline and attribution
- Regulatory reporting requirements

## 7. CHAIN OF CUSTODY & EVIDENCE PRESERVATION

### 7.1 Evidence Identification

All electronic evidence shall be uniquely identified with:
- **Chain of Custody (CoC) Number:** COC-2026-LOG-[XXXX]
- **Date and Time Received:** ISO 8601 format
- **Source Device/System:** Full identification
- **Hash Algorithm Verification:** MD5, SHA-1, SHA-256
- **Physical Description and Condition:** Detailed logging

### 7.2 Evidence Acquisition Methods:

Provider shall use forensically sound methods including:
- **Write-blocking hardware/software**
- **Bit-for-bit forensic imaging**
- **Live acquisition when necessary**
- **Encrypted storage media**
- **Cryptographic verification at all stages**

### 7.3 Chain of Custody Documentation:

For all evidence, Provider shall maintain:
- Complete Chain of Custody forms
- Access logs with timestamps
- Personnel authorized to handle evidence
- Storage location and environmental conditions
- Any transfer of custody documentation
- Disposition of evidence documentation

### 7.4 NIST Compliance:

Chain of custody procedures comply with:
- **NIST SP 800-86:** Integrating Forensic Techniques
- **NIST SP 800-101 Rev. 1:** Mobile Device Forensics
- **NIST SP 800-86:** Guide to Integrating Forensic Techniques
- **SWGDE Best Practices:** For Digital Forensics

### 7.5 Evidence Storage:

- **Secure, access-controlled facility:** ISO 27001 certified
- **Environmental monitoring:** Continuous logging
- **Backup and redundancy:** Redundant storage arrays
- **Access restricted to authorized personnel only:** Multi-factor authentication
- **Compliance with applicable warrant or subpoena requirements:** Legal review

### 7.6 Evidence Retention:

Unless otherwise specified in Work Order:
- **Original Evidence:** Retained minimum 90 days from case closure
- **Forensic working copies:** Retained minimum 90 days
- **Report copies:** Retained for minimum of 3 years
- **Long-term retention:** Available per additional agreement ($500/year)

### 7.7 Evidence Disposition:

Evidence shall be returned to Logitech or destroyed per:
- **Logitech written instructions**
- **Court orders or subpoena requirements**
- **Work Order specifications**
- **30-day notice period for unclaimed materials**
