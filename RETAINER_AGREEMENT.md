## 1. RECITALS

### 1.1 Background
This Master Digital Forensics Retainer Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into to establish terms and conditions under which Client may request and Provider shall perform digital forensics services. The parties intend to create a framework for future specific service engagements called "Work Orders."

### 1.2 Intent
This Agreement establishes that Provider is engaged to provide digital forensics services without creating an attorney-client relationship, joint venture, or partnership. All services will be delivered in accordance with industry standards and best practices.

### 1.3 Admissibility
The parties understand that this Agreement may be relied upon in litigation or administrative proceedings concerning the authentication and admissibility of digital evidence.

## 2. DEFINITIONS

### 2.1 "Digital Forensics Services"
Professional services including but not limited to:
- Computer forensics investigations
- Mobile device forensic analysis
- Network forensics and incident response
- Data recovery and retrieval
- Malware analysis
- Cloud forensics
- Memory forensics
- Email and communication analysis
- Digital evidence preservation

### 2.2 "Evidence"
All digital data, information, media, and materials collected, examined, or analyzed by Provider during performance of services under this Agreement.

### 2.3 "Chain of Custody"
The documented chronological record documenting the seizure, custody, control, transfer, analysis, and disposition of physical or electronic evidence.

### 2.4 "Work Order"
A written directive issued under this Master Agreement specifying particular services, scope, timeline, and fees for a discrete engagement.

### 2.5 "Investigation Report"
The written deliverable documenting Provider's findings, methodologies, and conclusions.

### 2.6 "Industry Standards"
Recognized standards including:
- NIST Special Publications (SP) 800-86, 800-101, 800-115, 800-160
- SWGDE (Scientific Working Group on Digital Evidence) Best Practices
- ISO/IEC 27037, 27041, 27042
- ACPO (Association of Chief Police Officers) Principles

## 4. EVIDENCE HANDLING AND CHAIN OF CUSTODY

### 4.1 Evidence Identification
All electronic evidence shall be uniquely identified with:
- Chain of Custody (CoC) Number
- Date and time received
- Source device/system
- Hash algorithm verification (MD5, SHA-1, SHA-256)
- Physical description and condition

### 4.2 Evidence Acquisition Methods
Provider shall use forensically sound methods including:
- Write-blocking hardware/software
- Bit-for-bit forensic imaging
- Live acquisition when necessary
- Encrypted storage media
- Cryptographic verification at all stages

### 4.3 Chain of Custody Documentation
For all evidence, Provider shall maintain:
- Complete Chain of Custody forms
- Access logs with timestamps
- Personnel authorized to handle evidence
- Storage location and environmental conditions
- Any transfer of custody documentation
- Disposition of evidence documentation

### 4.4 Evidence Storage
- Secure, access-controlled facility
- Environmental monitoring
- Backup and redundancy
- Access restricted to authorized personnel only
- Compliance with applicable warrant or subpoena requirements

### 4.5 Evidence Retention
Unless otherwise specified in Work Order:
- Original evidence: Retained minimum 90 days from case closure
- Forensic working copies: Retained minimum 90 days
- Report copies: Retained for a minimum of 3 years
- Long-term retention available per additional agreement

### 4.6 Evidence Disposition
Evidence shall be returned to Client or destroyed per:
- Client written instructions
- Court orders or subpoena requirements
- Work Order specifications
- 90-day notice period for unclaimed materials

- Documented sufficient for peer review

- Version control for forensic tools

- Tools validated for specific use case

### 5.6 Privileged Information Handling
